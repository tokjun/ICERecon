
3D Slicer Extension to reconstract 3D model of heart chamber from 2D intracardiac echo (ICE) images.

The extention consists of ICESim, which simulate 2D ICE imaging, and ICERecon, which reconstract a 3D model.
The extension can be implemented in Python for initial prototyping, but may be converted to C++ later if the performance becomes an issue.

ICESim takes two input nodes: (1) 3D segmentation of the heart chambers and vessels, and (2) a linear transform node that represents the position and orientation of the imaging plane, which is attached to the ICE probe. ICESim also takes sveral parameters, including matrix size (X and Y) and pixel spacing (sx, sy) of the simulated ultrasound. ICESim also needs one output scalar volume node.
ICESim generates a simulated ICE image by taking the cross section of the 3D segmentation along the imaging plane, and render a simulated ultrasound image, which shows dark pixel within the blood pool (heart chambers and blood vessles in the segmentaiton) and bright pixels in the tissue (outside the blood pool). Some white noises are added to the image to make it more realistic.
